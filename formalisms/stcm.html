Conservation claim

STCM states that admissible state transitions must preserve traceable receipt continuity across authority, evidence, decision, execution, and replay surfaces.

A transition is not legitimate merely because a state changed. A transition becomes publicly admissible only when the movement from prior state to next state can be reconstructed with enough authority, evidence, and receipt continuity to survive replay.

What STCM currently supports

STCM can currently support:
- receipt continuity doctrine
- replay legitimacy checks
- ingestion movement discipline
- commit-time admissibility reconstruction
- authority/evidence/decision separation
- refusal and fail-closed traceability
- public posture boundaries for state changes

STCM should not yet claim:
- standalone mathematical closure
- independent formal verification
- complete conservation equations
- production authority outside governed tests
- authority without receipts
