Financial execution should not become real until authority, evidence, and admissibility still hold.

StegFinCo is the governed financial execution layer for StegVerse. It applies Fin-Co constitutional constraints at the point of financial action, while Fin-Co-Lab independently certifies the behavior and StegPay supplies verified payment evidence without becoming authority.

How StegPay is wired in

StegPay is deliberately narrow: it verifies that a payment event happened. It does not decide what that payment authorizes.

Stripe or external payment event → StegPay verifies event integrity and required metadata → StegPay emits deterministic payment evidence → StegFinCo treats the event as evidence only → StegFinCo checks current authority and admissibility → ALLOW / DENY / FAIL_CLOSED → receipt written → downstream StegOps or accounting action may proceed only if admitted

Research support boundary

Support payments fund independent StegVerse research, demos, certification work, and open governance infrastructure. They do not create StegToken rights, StegCoin rights, governance access, investment upside, repayment claims, or automatic entitlement.

Boundary doctrine

Fin-Co defines. StegFinCo executes. Fin-Co-Lab certifies. StegPay verifies payment evidence. Payment is evidence. Governance creates authority. StegFinCo controls effect. No uncertain financial transition proceeds without FAIL_CLOSED.
