HIL-TRACE-0001

The thread in which the experiment became observable

During a public discussion of this thought experiment, Sara Katpar submitted the argument to her own language model. Her model described the human–LLM relationship as a reciprocal feedback loop: people shape prompts, models shape outputs, and those outputs influence later conversations.

Sara compared that response with an independently generated response from another person's model and observed that the same apparent task produced different interpretations reflecting different context, prompt history, and writing style. Her conclusion was that the thought experiment might not be hypothetical: the process was occurring inside the discussion itself.

Classification: initiating pre-protocol observational trace. It is not represented as a standardized protocol submission.

Permission state: named attribution, exchange reproduction, model-response description, paper inclusion, and public Site inclusion granted.

Review state: awaiting Sara Katpar's prepublication review and any requested correction of context, wording, attribution, or presentation.

What the experiment tests

The paper proposes that people carry information, context, authority, verification, and action between otherwise bounded machine processes. The protocol observes how independently selected LLMs interpret, criticize, transform, refuse, or extend the same canonical input.

Responses remain independent records. Recurrence is not treated as proof of consciousness, hidden intent, autonomous coordination, or scientific validation.
