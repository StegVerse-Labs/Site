## Candidate research title

**Deterministic Governance Infrastructure for Large-Scale Agentic AI Systems**

Alternate bounded title:

**Boundary-Conditioned Autonomy: Authority, Admissibility, and Reconstruction in Multi-Agent AI Systems**

## Research question

Can a distributed agentic AI workload preserve and independently reconstruct:

1. who or what possessed execution authority at commit time;
2. which policy and delegation artifacts were valid at that time;
3. whether the proposed transition was admissible;
4. whether execution remained inside declared actuator and resource boundaries;
5. whether later observers can deterministically replay or reconstruct the decision and evidence chain without trusting the original runtime's assertions?

## Proposed experimental workstreams

### Workstream A — Commit-time authority reconstruction

Run controlled agent tasks in which policy, identity, delegation, evidence freshness, or execution context changes before commit. Compare runtime assertions with independently reconstructed authority and admissibility results.

### Workstream B — Multi-agent boundary pressure

Simulate many cooperating and competing agents under bounded resources, changing authority, conflicting objectives, and degraded operator availability. Record pre-commit pressure, proposed transitions, denials, overrides, and recovery behavior.

### Workstream C — Deterministic replay and custody

Emit canonical manifests, evidence references, receipts, transition decisions, and post-state hashes. Test whether an independent verifier can reproduce ALLOW, DENY, or FAIL-CLOSED outcomes and distinguish replayability from reconstructability.

### Workstream D — Performance and scaling cost

Measure the compute, latency, storage, and coordination overhead introduced by governance checks across increasing agent counts and workload complexity.
