Admissible Existence

The root formalism for coherent persistence across admissible transition.

Research-stage notice: This page is exploratory, subject to revision, and not a production claim.

Core Thesis

Existence is not merely presence. Existence is coherent persistence across admissible transition.

A state, entity, observer, process, or domain exists admissibly when it can preserve coherent identity across transition while remaining recoverable, observable, and compositionally compatible with the reality that contains it.

Minimum Conditions

p > 0
c ≥ c_min
r ≥ r_min
o ≥ o_min
m ≥ m_min

Where p is presence, c is coherence, r is recoverability, o is observability, and m is compositional compatibility.
