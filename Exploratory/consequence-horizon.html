Consequence Horizon Formalism

The formal layer for transitions that become binding consequence.

Research-stage notice: This page is exploratory, subject to revision, and not a production claim.

Core Thesis

The commit boundary matters because it is the last controllable surface before a transition crosses the consequence horizon.

A consequence horizon is a boundary at which a transition becomes sufficiently binding that the originating system can no longer fully reverse, recover, observe, or reabsorb it without additional consequence.

Admissibility Condition

R(Φ(S,u)) ≥ R_min
O(Φ(S,u)) ≥ O_min
A(Φ(S,u)) ≥ A_min
K(Φ(S,u)) ≥ K_min

Recoverability, observability, absorption capacity, and coherence retention must remain above threshold before the transition is allowed to cross the horizon.
