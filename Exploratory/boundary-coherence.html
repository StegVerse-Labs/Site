Boundary-Coherence

The boundary architecture through which coherent existence becomes entity-like.

Research-stage notice: This page is exploratory, subject to revision, and not a production claim.

Core Thesis

A thing exists when a boundary allows it to remain coherent across transitions.

Boundary-Coherence studies how shells, membranes, horizons, fields, interfaces, and transition surfaces preserve identity while regulating exchange.

Boundary Load Condition

boundary_flux ≤ absorption_capacity

F_B(t) ≤ A_B(t)

Boundary overload begins when incoming transition load exceeds the interior capacity to absorb that transition without losing coherence.

Cross-Scale Pattern

Particles, atoms, molecules, cells, organisms, ecosystems, planets, solar systems, galaxies, black holes, institutions, and software systems all exhibit boundary-like structures. The claim is formal, not that these boundaries share one physical mechanism.
