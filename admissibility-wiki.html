AI-governed proposal system

The AI-governed proposal system is the wiki process by which user-submitted, maintainer-submitted, browser-originated, LLM-assisted, and AI-entity-suggested changes enter structured review.

A proposal may request a new term, revised definition, equivalence claim, overlap claim, adjacent-term mapping, implementation mapping, external reference, counterexample, dispute, or proof-path example.

Submission creates a proposal record and a submission receipt. It does not create acceptance authority. Acceptance, rejection, deferral, escalation, refusal, or supersession requires a decision record.

Submission receipt timing

User proposal receipts should include timing for the submission process itself: when the proposal was received, when the receipt was issued, which submission-stage tasks ran, and when each task started and completed.

Timing supports reconstructability. Timing does not prove the submitted claim, accept the proposal, or replace the decision record.

Irreversibility-Inference Convergence Theorem

The Irreversibility-Inference Convergence Theorem, or IICT, is a proposed theorem candidate under commit-time admissibility. It does not replace commit-time admissibility. Commit-time admissibility asks whether execution still has standing at the moment of commitment. IICT proposes a convergence behavior that may emerge when admissibility systems are repeatedly optimized and tested.

The core question is: how close should the final valid inference be to the first irreversible action?

Theorem candidate: a governance system subjected to repeated admissibility optimization will converge toward structures that minimize the distance between irreversible commitment and the final admissible inference while preserving reconstructability.

TermDefinition
Inference WindowThe interval in which authority, policy, evidence, context, delegation, and memory are still valid enough to support a standing determination.
Point of IrreversibilityThe moment after which a proposed action cannot be fully undone without residual consequence.
DistanceThe temporal, procedural, evidentiary, and authority gap between final admissible inference and irreversible commitment.
ConvergenceThe tendency of mature governance structures to compress that distance while preserving reconstruction evidence.
