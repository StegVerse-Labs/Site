The basic idea

You are not trying to prove everything at once. You are collecting enough reliable information to decide the safest next commercial step.

Simple example: A seller says a locomotive is a GP10 in good condition. The workspace does not accept that statement as proof. It records the claim, asks what source supports it, compares it with other records, and recommends whether to investigate, price cautiously, re-scope, reject, or advance for formal review.

Step 1: identifying the unit

Candidate IDYour own tracking name. Example: CAND-2026-001.
Unit or road numberThe number painted on or assigned to the locomotive. It may not uniquely identify its entire rebuild history.
Donor lineageThe original locomotive family before rebuilding, such as GP7 or GP9. Choose UNKNOWN when the record is uncertain.
Why this matters: Two locomotives both called “GP10” may have different original frames, electrical systems, modifications, and repair histories.

Step 2: describing what is known

Grade A: The unit appears suitable for a bounded standard build, based on meaningful evidence.
Grade B: The unit may still work, but uncertainty requires discovery work, allowances, contingencies, or cost-plus pricing.
Grade C: The unit is outside ordinary acceptance. It may be donor material, a research/custom project, or a rejection.

Evidence state means how much support exists for the current numbers and claims:

ASSUMPTION_ONLYSomeone believes it, but no dependable source has been preserved.
PARTIALLY_SOURCEDSome values have sources; important gaps remain.
EVIDENCE_BACKEDThe important claims have supporting records.
VERIFIED_ACTUALThe value has been confirmed as an actual result, not merely a forecast or quote.

Step 3: finding and importing records

Useful searches often begin with the unit number, reporting mark, serial number, owner, prior operator, shop, and approximate rebuild date.

Example search path:
1. Search the road number and reporting mark.
2. Look for roster pages, sale listings, inspection records, repair invoices, shop records, owner exports, or registry records.
3. Save the record or export it as JSON/CSV when authorized.
4. Record where it came from and who controls it.

Source owner role asks who controls the original record. Authority class asks how much weight that record should normally carry.

AUTHORITATIVE_REGISTRYAn industry registry responsible for that record class.
SYSTEM_OF_RECORDThe owner or operator system officially used to maintain that information.
SIGNED_INSPECTIONA named inspection record signed or attributable to a qualified reviewer.
SUPPLIER_RECORDA supplier quote, part record, or delivery statement.
EXPORTED_OPERATIONAL_RECORDAn authorized export from a maintenance or operating system.
MANUAL_ASSERTIONA person entered the claim without a stronger preserved source.
ASSUMPTIONA planning placeholder that must not be treated as fact.

What happens when records disagree?

Example conflict: A seller spreadsheet says the donor lineage is GP9, while a registry or historical record says GP7. The workspace keeps both values and creates a review item. It does not silently choose one.

A conflict does not automatically mean fraud or error. It means the difference must be resolved before relying on the field.

Identity conflicts—such as unit number, serial number, reporting mark, or donor lineage—receive the strongest warning because the wrong identity can contaminate every later decision.

Step 4: project numbers

Total costExpected full project cost.
Quote priceThe amount proposed to the customer.
ContingencyMoney reserved for foreseeable uncertainty.
Warranty reserveMoney reserved for expected warranty exposure.
Contribution costCost used to determine whether the project contributes positively before all allocated overhead.
Stop-loss exposureThe maximum loss presently exposed if the project goes badly.
Important: Missing numbers should remain missing. The workspace is designed to reveal uncertainty, not hide it behind automatic guesses.

What the final posture means

DISCOVERY_ONLYLearn more before making an ordinary commercial commitment.
COST_PLUSProceed only with pricing that protects against uncertain actual costs.
RE_SCOPEChange the work, price, assumptions, or exclusions before advancing.
REJECTDo not accept the project through the ordinary path.
PROCEEDThe record may advance to named governed review. It is not approval to execute.

Governance in one paragraph

Governance means the system keeps track of who supplied information, what authority the source actually has, what conflicts remain, what decision was produced, and what the decision does not authorize. The workspace may recommend a next step, but a recommendation is never silently converted into approval or execution authority.

Plain-language rule: A record can be organized, validated, stored, and reviewed without becoming permission to spend money, order parts, sign a customer agreement, or begin work.
