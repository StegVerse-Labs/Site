Verification principles

A receipt is not a log. It is a continuity proof. Verification means confirming the chain is unbroken — that each transition was admitted, that the evidence matches the declared inputs, and that no silent mutation occurred between steps.

A stage map entry marked complete is not proof of completion. The receipt chain is proof of completion. The stage map records the state. The receipts prove the transitions.

Site publication of receipts does not make Site the authority. The authority is the system that produced the receipts. Site is the mirror.
