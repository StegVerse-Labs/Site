RTG — Relational Transition Geometry

Relational Transition Geometry is the foundational model for describing the relationships among actor, target, authority, boundary, context, evidence, recoverability, inference window, coherency horizon, and point of irreversibility.

It comes before the Transition Table. The geometry defines the shape of transition relationships; the Transition Table operationalizes that shape into discrete cells and routes.

CHF — Coherency Horizon

Coherency Horizon is the boundary beyond which no additional information can be obtained from within a stabilized coherent state, even though existence may continue inside that boundary.

CHF separates existence from knowability. A state may still exist, but if usable information can no longer be retrieved or reconstructed, commit-time admissibility cannot rely on existence alone.

Boundary statement

Runtime governance remains an execution-facing application of transition geometry. It should not overwrite RTG as the foundation term.

Continuity handoff remains a related continuity concept, but CHF is reserved for Coherency Horizon.
